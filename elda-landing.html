The Challenge

Canada's immigrant elders face
challenges technology has ignored

A growing population of African diaspora elders in Canada carry irreplaceable cultural knowledge — stories, languages, proverbs, and traditions — with no accessible way to pass them on.

🌍

Social Isolation

Many elders live far from family abroad, lack cultural social networks in Canada, and have limited access to spaces where they feel at home.

🗣️

Language Barriers

English-first digital tools exclude elders whose primary languages are Swahili, Somali, Yoruba, Amharic, Tigrinya, and others.

📱

Digital Exclusion

Every app and platform is designed for younger, tech-literate users. Elders are consistently left behind by the tools built to connect people.

📖

Cultural Loss

When elders pass away without a way to share their knowledge, entire cultural memories disappear — stories, languages, and traditions gone forever.

Our Intellectual Foundation

A framework built on
three interconnected ideas

ELDA is not just a technology project. It is grounded in a carefully considered theoretical framework that explains why this problem exists and why our approach is different.

Foundation: Relational Knowledge Ethics

Knowledge is not extracted — it is held in relationship. Elder stories, languages, and cultural memory belong to living webs of connection, not databases. This is the operating logic behind every decision ELDA makes. It is Ubuntu in practice, not just in name.

1

Intergenerational Cultural Memory Theory

Jan Assmann

Societies maintain identity through stories, language, and ritual passed between generations. When the chain breaks — as it does in migration — identity fractures. ELDA repairs that chain.

2

Epistemic Injustice Applied to Diaspora Aging

Miranda Fricker

Immigrant elders' ways of knowing are rendered invisible by systems not built for them. The absence of culturally grounded technology is a structural failure, not a market gap.

3

Generativity in Later Life

Erik Erikson

Elders' wellbeing depends on being able to pass wisdom forward. When they cannot, isolation and decline follow. ELDA is not just a cultural tool — it is a health intervention.

In plain language

Cultural Memory Theory means...

Think about what happens when a grandfather's stories about the village, the migration, the family history disappear with him. That knowledge lived only in him. The chain broke. ELDA is designed to help repair that chain before it breaks.

Epistemic Injustice means...

Most technology tools were designed by and for people in English-speaking, Western contexts. When an elder who speaks Somali or Tigrinya tries to use those tools, they don't work — not because of the elder, but because the elder was never considered. ELDA considers them first.

Generativity in Aging means...

One of the most deeply human needs as we grow older is the drive to leave something behind — to pass on what we know, what we've lived, what we believe. When elders can't do this, it affects their health. ELDA gives elders a dignified way to do exactly that.

Our Methodology

The Elder Wisdom Circle

Instead of traditional focus groups, ELDA is building something more relational. A small group of trusted elders from different African diaspora communities in Ottawa who co-design the research alongside us.

Traditional Focus Groups
✗ One-time sessions — 60 to 90 minutes
✗ Participants don't feel ownership
✗ Harder to build trust with elders
✗ Data collected, community rarely benefits
✗ Researcher extracts, community gives
ELDA Elder Wisdom Circle
✓ 6 to 8 elders — ongoing over months
✓ Elders co-design the research
✓ Trust builds over time — depth of insight
✓ Findings shared back with participants
✓ Community members earn honorariums
"One elder from each target community — a trusted voice, a community connector, a holder of cultural knowledge."
Our Commitment

Built on ethics, not just values

African and Black communities have often been asked to give — their data, their stories, their time — without seeing anything come back. ELDA is built to be structurally different.

🤝

Reciprocity

Every interaction gives something before it asks for anything. Elders and community organizations are partners, not sources of data.

🛡️

Community Ownership

Elders own their own stories. Their recordings, their words, their cultural knowledge do not become ELDA's property. Full stop.

💡

Transparency

ELDA is eventually a social enterprise with a product. We say that openly. There are no hidden agendas about how this work will evolve.

📋

Findings Shared Back

Every participating organization receives a summary of what we learned. The community benefits from its own contribution to this research.

💰

Honorariums

Elder participants are compensated for their time. Their knowledge has value, and we treat it that way from the very first conversation.

🌱

Anti-Extraction

We do not study communities from the outside. We build with them from the inside. That is not a tagline — it is a design principle.
